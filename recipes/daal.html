Daal

Description

Daal is a popular South Asian dish enjoyed throughout the world. Daal is an umbrella term for a variety of different pulses and lentils as well as the cooked soup-like dish. However, especially in diasporic communities, the cooked dish of daal is made with split mung bean lentils.

The preparation of the dish varies from family and communinity. If you do follow this recipe the first time and enjoy it, I highly recommend you adjust it the next time you make it to add a personal twist!

Also, daal is usually made with a pressure cooker, but it is not necessary. This recipe does not need a pressure cooker.

Ingredients

For the Daal itself:

  • 2 cups of uncooked yellow mung bean lentils
  • 3 tomatoes
  • Half of a large yellow onion
  • A thumb-sized knob of ginger
  • About 6-7 cloves of garlic
  • A teaspoon each of:
    • Ground cumin
    • Kashmiri red chili powder
    • Ground coriander
    • Paprika, for color
  • Salt, to taste

For the tempered spices:

  • A teaspoon of whole cumin
  • A dash of heeng/asafoetida
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4-5 Curry leaves

Steps

  1. Wash and drain the lentils
  2. Blend the onion, tomatoes, garlic and ginger with 1/3 cup water.
  3. Over a medium-high flame, add washed 2 cups of lentils to about 3 cups of water
  4. Add in the ground cumin, Kashmiri red chili powder, ground coriander, paprika, and salt.
  5. Add in the blended mixture of onions, tomatoes, garlic and ginger.
  6. Cook covered for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Once desired consistency is reached, remove from flame and set to a side burner.
  8. Prepare a frying pan with about 2 tablespoons of oil
  9. Once the oil is hot, add in the minced garlic.
  10. When garlic begins to turn golden-brown, quickly add the whole cumin and heeng.
  11. Fry the ingredients for 30 seconds, then toss in the curry leaves and turn off the flame.
  12. Pour the tempered oil over the daal and stir.
  13. Taste and adjust for salt.
  14. Garnish with cilantro. Serve with white basmati rice and raita/yogurt.
